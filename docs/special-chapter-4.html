The Little Things I'll Never Forget

ₚ·.°.☆•

Memory is strange.

Ask me what we talked about on the fifth day, and I probably couldn't tell you.

Ask me the exact order of our conversations, and I'd get it wrong.

But ask me what I remember...

and suddenly the smallest things become impossible to forget.

Not the milestones.

The moments.

Those ordinary little moments that quietly became part of me.

✦

I'll remember your "good mornings."

Not because they were poetic.

Most of them weren't.

Sometimes they were just a sleepy greeting before work.

Sometimes they came with a picture.

Sometimes they came late because life was already happening around you.

But somehow...

they became the first smile of my day.

I didn't realize how much comfort could fit inside two ordinary words.

Good morning.

✦

I'll remember how naturally you became part of my routine.

I'd finish work and instinctively reach for my phone.

Not because I needed entertainment.

Because I wanted to know how your day went.

Whether the kids had already eaten.

Whether work had been stressful.

Whether you'd remembered to eat yourself.

I don't know when concern quietly replaced curiosity.

I only know it happened.

✦

I'll remember coffee.

It's funny.

Coffee existed long before you.

It still exists now.

But every time I make one, there's still a tiny part of me that remembers the mornings we happened to be drinking ours together.

Different houses.

Different mugs.

Different lives.

Yet somehow it felt like we were sharing the same table.

Coffee tastes different when someone becomes part of the ritual.

✦

I'll remember how often we laughed.

Not because either of us was particularly funny.

Most of our jokes would've made absolutely no sense to anyone else.

They were ours.

The kind of jokes that grow naturally when two people spend enough ordinary time together.

Inside jokes are funny like that.

They're proof that two people slowly built a tiny language no one else speaks.

We had one.

Maybe more than one.

And I hope somewhere inside you...

some of those jokes still make you smile too.

✦

I'll remember your children.

Not because I spent years watching them grow.

I didn't.

But because every story you told carried the same thing.

Love.

You never had to convince me they mattered.

I could hear it every time you talked about them.

Maybe that's why loving you never felt separate from caring about them.

You were never just one person.

You were already a family.

And strangely...

that never scared me.

It made me admire you even more.

✦

I'll never forget the evening your eldest daughter kissed me on the cheek.

It happened so quickly.

No dramatic music.

No grand announcement.

She simply decided I was safe.

Children don't fake that.

They don't overthink it.

They just know.

Out of everything that happened between us...

that tiny kiss became one of the biggest memories I carried away.

I still smile when I think about it.

Sometimes I still cry too.

Both are okay.

✦

I'll remember the way you called me "hon."

It's such a small word.

People say it every day.

But words are different once they're attached to a specific voice.

Even now...

sometimes when I read our old conversations...

I don't read that word.

I hear it.

Memory has a voice.

Yours still lives in mine.

✦

I'll remember the pictures.

The random selfies.

The food.

The moments that didn't need to be photographed but were anyway.

Looking at them now doesn't make me wish I could go back.

It makes me grateful that, for a little while...

I got to witness your ordinary life.

There's something beautiful about ordinary life.

Most people don't realize it while they're living it.

✦

I'll remember how easy it felt to imagine tomorrow.

Not because tomorrow was guaranteed.

Because you made it feel possible.

There's a difference.

One is certainty.

The other is hope.

Hope was enough.

✦

Maybe one day...

these memories won't arrive as often.

Maybe years from now I'll struggle to remember exactly how one conversation went.

That's okay.

Memory was never meant to preserve everything.

Only the things our hearts refuse to lose.

And if life slowly asks me to forget the details...

I hope it lets me keep the feeling.

Warm.

Safe.

Hopeful.

Loved.

Because that's who you were inside my memories.

Not perfect.

Not forever.

Just...

someone who made fifteen ordinary days feel extraordinary.

✦

Some people leave behind photographs.

Some leave behind letters.

You left behind something quieter.

A version of myself that remembered how beautiful ordinary life could be.

I think...

that's something I'll always be thankful for.

✦

— Ralph.
