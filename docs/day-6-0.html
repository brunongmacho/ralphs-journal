The Day Everything Is Ordinary

ₚ·.°.☆•

This morning felt ordinary.

I don't think I've ever appreciated ordinary this much.

I woke up at 7:53. Checked my phone — emails, time, messages. Doom-scrolled Facebook for a bit. My cousin messaged about the house bills, so I paid them. Adulting. Got out of bed, fixed my bed, cleaned a bit in the kitchen. Went to the bathroom, did my business, cleaned myself up. Then I opened my PC and logged the first entry of Day 6.0.

A week ago, the journal was keeping me afloat.

Today, it simply came after my morning routine.

That's a small shift.

But I think that's how healing quietly announces itself.

✦

I washed the dishes while my phone kept buzzing. It was Jalo.

"Ang haba ah mamaya ko tuloy iba hahaha. Pero ganda neto a. Kasi na eexpress mo."

I told him that's exactly it — "Ung mga binibitbit ko atleast nasulat ko. Gumaan. D ko na kailangan bitbitin ng bitbitin."

He asked if I made it on GitHub. Then he joked, "Malay mo bukas APK na yan." Classic Jalo.

Sabi ni Chie dami daw eh puro PDF nung una. So I made it into one website. Easier.

✦

I left a message for my mom too.

Sent her the link. Told her I think I'm okay now. "Nalabas ko na lahat sa sulat. Talent mo to eh. Salamat at pinahiram mo sakin nanay Liza. I love you so much. I miss you."

I meant it.

She's the reason words have always felt like home.

I've always known that.

Today, I finally told her.

✦

After the dishes, I played some Jack Johnson and made coffee.

I didn't do anything.

I just sat there with my coffee while Jack Johnson played in the background. No urgency. No emotional emergency. Just a Monday morning.

Started watching a K-drama — The Legend of Kitchen Soldier. It's funny. Like Solo Leveling but in cooking. While watching, I cooked Pancit Canton. Extra spicy ones. So I could enjoy the show better.

Then I remembered — it's Monday. My first day of retraining. 11 PM to 7 AM. I got benched a while back, so this is me starting over. Nothing exciting there. But I'm curious how different it'll feel compared to a year and a half ago.

✦

At 10 AM, the secretary of my uncle's company messaged me. Asked if I still remembered the carrot cutter I designed. She needs the blades to be detachable so they can fabricate them.

It's nice to know people still remember my work.

Sometimes life reminds you that while you've been busy healing, the world never forgot what you're capable of.

I told myself I'll do the designing later. The K-drama was more important right now.

✦

At 11 AM I caught myself falling asleep while watching. So I took a cold shower. Nothing beats a cold shower in the afternoon.

While taking a bath, I played some music and danced. I'm a good dancer — but only inside the bathroom. The music was Kamikazee. Upbeat. It woke me right up.

After that, I cut my nails, shaved, did the other small stuff. Then I sat down to write this.

✦

Today wasn't memorable because something extraordinary happened.

It was memorable because nothing needed saving.

The dishes still needed washing.

My nails still needed cutting.

The carrot cutter still needed redesigning.

Training still starts at eleven tonight.

Bills still had to be paid.

Coffee still tasted better while Jack Johnson played in the background.

Life didn't pause to congratulate me for healing.

It simply continued.

And for the first time in a long while...

I was happy to continue with it.

✦

Today's Weight: 8/10

Not because anything extraordinary happened — but because nothing needed saving.

There's a different kind of peace in that.

A week ago I was surviving.

Today...

I just lived.

And somehow, that felt like progress.

Songs of Day 6.0

- Jack Johnson playlist — Monday morning coffee

- Kamikazee — bathroom dance session

✦

I don't think I'll remember today because something big happened.

I'll remember it because nothing big had to happen.

Life finally felt ordinary again.

And after everything...

ordinary feels extraordinary.

— Ralph.
