# Journal — Day 8
## July 1, 2026
### The Tomorrow I Thought Would Never Come

---

July began without announcing itself.

There were no fireworks. No dramatic countdown. No deep reflection as midnight passed. I was just... working. Designing websites and funnels. Recording another mock founder meeting for practice. The same things I'd been doing all week.

The calendar simply changed. And I didn't notice until much later.

✦
**5:41 AM** — Done with my tasks. Just waiting for my shift to end.

NoThingz is still playing in the background. I caught myself thinking about adding music to the journal website — not autoplay, just a little bookmark for anyone who wants to hear what I was listening to while writing these pages.

But then I remembered we already have Songs of the Day.

That's enough.

✦
**6:38 AM** — Dewy messaged me on Discord.

We started with our usual banter — I teased her about being a construction worker, she clarified she works with modular cabinets, hotel projects, even the Manila Polo Club. Then the conversation shifted.

She asked about my work.

I told her about Doneverse. How I got in without VA experience. How I'm currently on the bench after my client left, but instead of just sitting around, the company put me back into paid training. For a moment, I realized I wasn't just talking about my own journey. I was encouraging someone else to take theirs.

I told her to check the company website. That I'd even refer her.

*"Basta marunong ka mag-computer at marunong ka sumunod ng instruction... malamang papasa ka dito."*

*"Fake it till you make it lang."*

Reading those words now made me laugh. Not because they were funny. Because a week ago, I was the one questioning if I was even good enough. Tonight, I was convincing someone else that they were.

She joked about working HR during the day and being a VA at night. *"What if natanggap ako? Sa umaga HR ako, sa gabi VA."*

We both laughed.

Then she asked why Jalo and the others were inviting me to drink next weekend.

*"...kasi alam nila may pinagdaanan ako last week."*

She paused.

*"Are you okay, kuya?"*

I couldn't help but laugh. *"Thank you for that wonderful question."*

Then came the obvious follow-up. *"Broken ka??!!"*

Instead of trying to summarize everything through chat, I did something I never imagined I'd do. I sent her my journal.

*"Hirap explain eh."* *"Mahilig ka naman magbasa diba."*

A week ago, those pages only existed because I needed somewhere to pour everything. Now I'm sharing them with people. Not because I need them to understand me. But because maybe someone else might find something in them too.

Before the conversation ended, she casually mentioned something that surprised me. She and Gasm had already broken up last year.

I honestly didn't know.

For a moment, the roles almost reversed. She was about to read my story while carrying one of her own.

Maybe that's what I've slowly been realizing lately. Everyone is quietly carrying something. Some people just hide it better than others.

✦
**7:00 AM** — Shift over.

I'm just sleepy. Not emotionally drained. Not mentally overwhelmed. Just sleepy.

I think that's the biggest difference between the Ralph from last week and the Ralph writing this now. One was exhausted from carrying pain. The other is simply tired from living.

✦
**1:18 PM** — Just woke up. Or, more accurately, I woke up an hour ago.

The first hour disappeared exactly how you'd expect — Facebook doom-scrolling. Nothing important. Nothing memorable. Just enough random posts and memes to convince my brain that yes, I'm awake now.

But today's different. Yesterday belonged to the rain. Today belongs to the sun.

The house feels brighter. I caught myself just... enjoying the weather. Not thinking about anything. Just noticing it.

I sent Dewy another message before putting my phone down.

**"Sooo... nabasa mo na?"**

She's probably still at work. I don't expect a reply anytime soon. But I'm strangely curious. Not because I need validation. I'm just wondering what it feels like for someone else to step into the pages I've been living inside these past few days.

✦
**1:28 PM** — I finally got up and cleaned myself up.

Lunch was simple. I turned yesterday's leftover rice into fried rice, opened a can of tuna, and called it a meal. Now I'm about to continue watching my K-drama, with another cup of coffee beside me.

No plans. No rush. Just another quiet afternoon.

✦
**3:44 PM** — Jalo started streaming *Meccha Chameleon* on Discord voice.

I stopped my K-drama and joined to watch. I was laughing almost immediately. It's his first time playing, and watching him confidently paint himself into disaster never gets old. His paint level? 0.0001.

For a while, I completely forgot everything else. We were just laughing at how terrible he was.

✦
**4:46 PM** — Jalo stopped playing. I went back to my K-drama.

Nothing much happened after that. Just another episode. Another cup of coffee still sitting beside me. Another peaceful afternoon.

✦
**7:35 PM** — Jalo streamed *Meccha Chameleon* again. Somehow it was just as funny the second time.

I don't know if the game is actually that funny...

or if watching a friend discover it for the first time is what makes it hilarious. Either way, I was laughing again. It's easier when someone else is there laughing with you.

✦
**9:55 PM** — Still on Discord. Jalo is watching something else now. Five more minutes before I log into my shift.

A week ago, I would've spent this hour rereading old conversations. Tonight, I spent it laughing over a ridiculous video game.

I think that's another kind of progress.

✦
**10:28 PM** — Joined our Zoom meeting for tonight's shift. Just waiting for our DSL to send the attendance sheet. At 11 PM, training starts again.

Tonight's topic: Social Media + Email Marketing. Founder Projects.

Eight days ago, I thought nights would always belong to heartbreak. Tonight, they belong to work. And I'm genuinely curious about what I'll learn next.

There was a time when logging in felt like something I had to force myself to do. Now it just feels normal again.

✦
### Today's Weight: 8/10

I worked.

I slept.

I cooked.

I laughed.

I helped someone.

I showed someone my story.

I remembered that I'm capable of being useful to people again.

I started July not with a resolution,
but with an ordinary day.

One week ago, this was the tomorrow I thought would never come.

I was wrong.
